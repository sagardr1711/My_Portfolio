Documentary and short video making

Telling real stories through visual media is where my passion meets purpose. I am deeply interested in capturing moments, voices, and themes that often go unnoticed—whether it's cultural traditions, social realities, or emotional human experiences. My journey into documentary and short video making began with a simple curiosity and has evolved into a commitment to using film as a tool for storytelling, awareness, and connection. I conceptualize and script original ideas, handle the filming process, and edit the footage to produce short films and documentaries that are both visually and emotionally engaging. While I am still learning and growing in this field, I have hands-on experience with DaVinci Resolve and Adobe Premiere Pro, and I use these tools to create clean, thoughtful edits. My work often includes color grading, sound design, voiceovers, subtitles, and music layering to enhance narrative tone and flow. One of my favorite themes to explore is culture, such as documenting ritualistic art forms like Theyyam, where tradition, spirituality, and performance converge in powerful ways. I also enjoy working on videos that bring attention to social issues, silent struggles, or overlooked perspectives. I believe even short videos have the power to spark thought, create empathy, and inspire change. Though I am not a certified professional, I approach each project with sincerity, creativity, and a desire to improve. Each film I make is a step forward in my learning journey—an opportunity to refine my craft and deliver stories that matter. My goal is simple: to create meaningful content that leaves an impression. Whether it’s a 60-second short or a 10-minute documentary, I strive to connect people through storytelling that is raw, relevant, and real.
